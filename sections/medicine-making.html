1. Oral Rehydration Solution — Start Here

Beginner 72hr — Make Now

Diarrhoeal diseases have killed more humans than almost any other cause in history. They kill through dehydration, not the infection itself. ORS replaces lost fluid and electrolytes. This single formula has saved more lives than any other medical intervention except vaccination.

💧 WHO Oral Rehydration Solution (ORS)

1 litre
CLEAN WATER
(boiled & cooled)
6 tsp
SUGAR
(level teaspoons)
½ tsp
SALT
(non-iodised ideal)

Stir until completely dissolved

PatientAmount after each loose stool
Adults200–400ml (1–2 cups)
Children 2–10 yr100–200ml (½–1 cup)
Infants <2 yr50–100ml (¼–½ cup)

Signs of improvement: urination resumes · skin loses tented appearance · mouth becomes moist · alertness returns

Needs IV fluids if: cannot keep ORS down · unconscious or severely altered · rapid weak pulse · sunken eyes, no tears, dry mouth

2. Tinctures — Alcohol-Based Plant Extracts

Intermediate 4–6 Week Preparation

A tincture is a plant extract preserved in alcohol. Alcohol extracts active compounds more completely than water and preserves them for 2–5 years. Standard ratio: 1 part dried herb to 5 parts alcohol (1:5). Minimum 40% alcohol (vodka); 60–70% ideal.

3. Salves & Poultices

Beginner 4–6 Week Oil Infusion

Basic Salve Formula

  1. Infuse herbs in olive oil: fill jar with dried herb, cover with olive oil, seal, leave in warm place 4–6 weeks (or heat gently for 4–8 hours at 40°C)
  2. Strain oil through muslin; discard plant material
  3. Melt 30g beeswax per 1 cup infused oil (low heat — beeswax burns)
  4. Mix oil and wax; test consistency by dropping on cold surface — add more wax to harden, more oil to soften
  5. Pour into tins or small jars while liquid. Shelf life: 1 year in cool, dark storage

6. Fever Management

Beginner 72hr

Physical Cooling Protocol (No Drugs Needed)

  1. Cool (not cold) damp cloths to forehead, armpits, and groin — these are the major heat exchange points
  2. Do NOT use cold water or ice: causes shivering which generates heat and raises temperature further
  3. Remove excess clothing and blankets
  4. Increase fluid intake — continuous small sips
  5. Reduce ambient room temperature if possible

Herbal Support

  • Yarrow tea: 1 tsp dried herb steeped 10 minutes; causes sweating which cools the body; 3× daily
  • Elderflower tea: same action as yarrow; pleasant taste
  • Willow bark: for fever reduction (see pain section)
🚨
Fever Emergencies
  • Fever + stiff neck + rash: meningitis — life-threatening emergency
  • Above 40°C in an infant: act immediately; give ORS; apply cool cloths; seek medical care
  • Febrile seizure in a child: do not restrain; protect from injury; time the seizure; place on side; cool the child after
  • Fever that doesn't respond to cooling above 41°C: immerse in cool (not cold) water bath

7. Antibiotic Alternatives

Intermediate Some Available Now
⚠️
Critical Limitation

The preparations below are NOT equivalent to pharmaceutical antibiotics for serious infections. Sepsis (blood poisoning), pneumonia, and severe wound infections can kill within hours. These preparations support early-stage infection and mild conditions only. If you have pharmaceutical antibiotics, use them for serious infections. The plants and preparations below may provide benefit for mild infections, surface wounds, and as adjuncts — not replacements for life-threatening scenarios.

Beginner Learn Now — Before Crisis 🌿 Temperate (UK & Western Europe)
☠️
Plant Identification Safety

Correct identification is critical. Many medicinal plants have toxic lookalikes. If you are not certain of identification, do not use the plant. Learn plants from a qualified teacher or verified field guide before relying on this text alone. When in doubt: do not use.

9. Quick Reference Card

🌿 Medicine Making Quick Reference

ORS (most important): 1L clean water + 6 level tsp sugar + ½ tsp salt. Give continuously during diarrhoea.

Tincture base ratio: 1 part dried herb : 5 parts 60% alcohol. Macerate 4–6 weeks, strain, bottle.

Basic salve: Infuse herb in olive oil 4–6 wks. Strain. Melt in 30g beeswax per cup oil. Pour into tins.

Wound care priority:

  1. Irrigate with saline (1L boiled water + 9g salt) under pressure
  2. Remove all debris and foreign matter
  3. Apply raw honey dressing
  4. Change dressing daily; watch for spreading redness (cellulitis)
  5. If spreading redness or fever develops: consider antibiotic alternatives above

Fever action: Below 39°C — monitor. 39–40°C — yarrow tea, cool cloths. Above 40.5°C — physical cooling immediately. Above 41.5°C — cold water immersion, life-threatening.

This card is not medical advice. Use pharmaceutical treatment when available. ⚡ Last Light Survival Guide
