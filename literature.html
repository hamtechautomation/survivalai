Food, Water & Agriculture (12 books)
The Encyclopedia of Country Living
Carla Emery — 10th ed., 2012
CRITICAL Agriculture Beginner
The most comprehensive single-volume homesteading reference ever written. 900+ pages covering gardening, grain growing, food preservation, livestock, butchering, dairy, soap making, candle making, fiber arts, and more. Carla Emery wrote it from her own experience living off the land. If you can only have one non-medical book, this is it.
Read first: Chapter 1 (Self-Sufficient Garden), Chapter 3 (Grain), Chapter 8 (Preserving), Chapter 11 (Livestock)
Gardening When It Counts: Growing Food in Hard Times
Steve Solomon — 2006
CRITICAL Agriculture Beginner
Written explicitly for low-input, no-inputs-available gardening. Covers soil fertility without commercial fertilizers, water-wise planting, open-pollinated seed selection, and maximizing caloric yield per square foot. Solomon is honest about what will fail and what will work when you can't run to the garden center. The most practically useful single gardening book.
Read first: Chapter 2 (Soil), Chapter 5 (Vegetables Worth Growing), Chapter 9 (Seed Saving)
The Humanure Handbook
Joseph Jenkins — 3rd ed., 2005
CRITICAL Agriculture FREE PDF Beginner
Safe human waste management without flush toilets is a critical public health issue that most preppers ignore until it's too late. Jenkins explains the thermophilic composting system that eliminates pathogens, creates high-quality compost, and prevents waterborne disease — the single most common killer in collapsed civilizations. Free PDF legally available from the author.
Read first: Chapter 3 (Pathogens), Chapter 5 (The Toilet System), Chapter 9 (Composting)

Suzanne Ashworth — 2002
CRITICAL Agriculture Intermediate
Seed saving closes the loop on agriculture — without it, you'll exhaust your seed supply in 1–2 seasons. This book covers isolation distances, hand-pollination, fermentation cleaning, drying, and storage for 160 vegetable species. Essential for multi-year food security. Pair with maintaining an open-pollinated seed bank before you need it.
Read first: Chapter 1 (Why Save Seeds), the species chapters for your staple crops
Four-Season Harvest
Eliot Coleman — 1999
IMPORTANT Agriculture Intermediate
How to grow fresh vegetables through winter without heating greenhouses — using cold frames and low tunnels. Coleman demonstrates year-round harvests in Maine (Zone 5). Critical for nutrition in northern climates where winter is the most dangerous period. Covers cold-hardy crop varieties and succession planting timing for continuous harvest.
Root Cellaring: Natural Cold Storage of Fruits & Vegetables
Mike & Nancy Bubel — 1991
IMPORTANT Agriculture Beginner
Root cellaring extends harvest across the entire non-growing season without canning, freezing, or any energy input. This book covers building or adapting a root cellar, optimal temperature/humidity requirements for 50+ crops, and harvest timing for maximum storage life. Often overlooked, root cellaring may be the most energy-efficient long-term food storage method.
Preserving Food Without Freezing or Canning
Collectif Terre Vivante — 2007
IMPORTANT Agriculture Beginner
Traditional European methods for food preservation that require no electricity, no store-bought supplies, and minimal equipment. Covers lacto-fermentation, drying, salt curing, oil packing, vinegar pickling, and cold cellaring. 250+ recipes gathered from French farming communities. The techniques here predate the Industrial Revolution and will outlast it.
Small-Scale Grain Raising

IMPORTANT Agriculture Intermediate
Grains provide the bulk of human caloric intake. This book covers growing wheat, corn, barley, rye, oats, buckwheat, and sunflowers on a small farm scale without commercial combines. Includes threshing, winnowing, grinding, and storage. A community without grain production is dependent on external supply chains; a community with it is genuinely self-sufficient.
Storey's Guide to Raising Chickens

IMPORTANT Agriculture Beginner

The Market Gardener
Jean-Martin Fortier — 2014
NICE TO HAVE Agriculture Intermediate
High-intensity bed farming that produces extraordinary yields per square foot without heavy machinery. Fortier's methods are directly applicable to community food production in a post-collapse scenario. Shows that skilled intensive gardening can feed far more people per acre than conventional farming.

Security & Tactics (8 books)
SAS Survival Handbook
John "Lofty" Wiseman — 3rd ed., 2009
CRITICAL Security Beginner
The definitive individual survival handbook from a 26-year SAS veteran. Covers shelter, fire, water, food, navigation, signaling, first aid, and survival in every climate from arctic to desert to jungle. Well-illustrated, organized for quick reference under stress. Different from most survival books in that it actually reflects hard-won field experience rather than theoretical knowledge.

US Army Ranger Handbook (SH 21-76)

Military small-unit tactics, patrol planning, mission planning, and field leadership. More operationally specific than FM 21-76. Left of Bang: How the Marine Corps' Combat Hunter Program Can Save Your Life
Patrick Van Horne & Jason Riley — 2014
IMPORTANT Security Beginner
Teaches the Marine Corps' Combat Hunter behavioral detection methodology for civilian application. How to read environmental anomalies, baseline behavior, kinesic cues, and pre-attack indicators before violence occurs. The "left of bang" title refers to getting your information before the triggering event. Practical, field-tested, and readable by non-military audiences. Potentially life-saving situational awareness skills.
Meditations on Violence
Rory Miller — 2008
IMPORTANT Security Intermediate
A corrections officer and martial artist examines the reality of violence versus how it is portrayed in training and media. Essential reading for anyone who may need to make decisions under violent threat — covers the difference between asocial and social violence, predator selection criteria, the dynamics of real attacks, and why most self-defense training fails under stress.
Total Resistance
Major H. von Dach — 1958
IMPORTANT Security Intermediate
Swiss military manual on civilian guerrilla resistance — originally written to prepare the Swiss civilian population for resistance in case of occupation. Covers organization, security, sabotage, evasion, and living covertly under hostile control. Historically important and practically relevant for communities that may face occupation or oppression in a collapse scenario.
The Art of War
Sun Tzu — ~500 BCE
IMPORTANT Security FREE PDF Beginner
2,500 years old and still the foundational text on strategy, deception, and resource management in conflict. The value is not tactics but principles: know yourself and your enemy; win without fighting when possible; strategic deception; speed and maneuver versus attrition. 13 short chapters that repay re-reading over years.

Forging the Hero
John Mosby (Mountain Guerrilla) — 2016
NICE TO HAVE Security Intermediate
From a former Special Forces soldier-turned-homesteader. Covers building community defense capability, the warrior ethos, tribalism, and realistic assessments of what small communities can and cannot accomplish in security situations. More philosophy and framework than tactics manual; pairs well with the Ranger Handbook for a balanced perspective.
Contact! A Tactical Manual for Post Collapse Survival
Max Velocity — 2012
NICE TO HAVE Security Advanced
Written by a British combat veteran for civilian audiences contemplating security operations in a grid-down environment. Covers patrol formations, reaction to contact, ambush, and small-unit leadership. More operationally specific than most civilian security books. Requires a foundation in weapons handling and physical fitness to apply.
Psychology & Community (8 books)
Deep Survival: Who Lives, Who Dies, and Why
Laurence Gonzales — 2003
CRITICAL Psychology Beginner
The best book written on the psychology of survival. Gonzales investigates why some people live and others die in identical situations — it has almost nothing to do with physical fitness or survival skills, and almost everything to do with mental and emotional characteristics. Based on neuroscience, accident reports, and survival case studies. Should be the first book you read. Will change how you approach every other topic.
Read all of it. Particularly Chapter 12 (Twelve Traits of Survivors) and Chapter 14 (The Rules of Adventure)
Man's Search for Meaning
Viktor E. Frankl — 1946
CRITICAL Psychology Beginner
A psychiatrist's account of surviving four Nazi concentration camps and the psychological insights derived from that experience. Develops the concept of logotherapy — that finding meaning enables humans to endure almost anything. The most important psychological text for people facing extended catastrophe. Short (under 200 pages). Read it now, not after.
Read first: Part One (Experiences in a Concentration Camp) — the most important pages
A Paradise Built in Hell: The Extraordinary Communities That Arise in Disaster
Rebecca Solnit — 2009
IMPORTANT Psychology Beginner
Examines how communities actually respond to disasters — and finds that the "every man for himself" panic that media portrays is largely a myth. People consistently become generous, cooperative, and purposeful in disasters. San Francisco 1906, Halifax 1917, Mexico City 1985, 9/11 NYC. Essential counter-narrative to apocalyptic prepper culture. Community is your greatest survival asset.
Read first: Introduction, Chapter 1 (San Francisco 1906), Chapter 9 (The Sociology of Catastrophe)
Tribe: On Homecoming and Belonging
Sebastian Junger — 2016
IMPORTANT Psychology Beginner
Examines why humans are psychologically adapted to tribal living — shared hardship, mutual obligation, and close community — and why modern society makes people miserable by eliminating these structures. The London Blitz paradox: mental illness rates dropped during the bombing. Provides a framework for understanding why rebuilding community bonds is as important as stockpiling supplies.
The Unthinkable: Who Survives When Disaster Strikes and Why
Amanda Ripley — 2008
IMPORTANT Psychology Beginner
Investigative journalist examines the human response to disasters — the 30-second "deer in headlights" freeze, why some people panic and others act, how crowds behave in fires and floods, and what training actually changes behavior under extreme stress. Based on interviews with survivors of disasters including 9/11, plane crashes, and Hurricane Katrina. Deeply practical.
The Art of Community
Charles Vogl — 2016
IMPORTANT Psychology Beginner
Practical guide to building and maintaining intentional communities. Covers membership criteria, shared rituals, leadership succession, conflict resolution, and community boundaries. More practical than theoretical — drawn from diverse community types from religious groups to open-source software projects. Directly applicable to forming and managing a survival group before and during a crisis.
Bowling Alone: The Collapse and Revival of American Community
Robert D. Putnam — 2000
NICE TO HAVE Psychology Intermediate
Comprehensive sociological analysis of the decline of social capital (trust, networks, civic participation) in American life over the 20th century. Explains why Americans today have weaker community ties than at any previous point — and why this is a survival liability. The first step to rebuilding community is understanding how it was lost.
Sources of Power: How People Make Decisions
Gary Klein — 1998
NICE TO HAVE Psychology Advanced
Research into how firefighters, military commanders, and intensive care nurses make decisions under time pressure, uncertainty, and high stakes. Introduces Recognition-Primed Decision making (RPD) — how experts actually decide, versus how textbooks say they should. Valuable for anyone who will be in a leadership or decision-making role in an emergency.
History & Case Studies (4 books)
Hiroshima
John Hersey — 1946
CRITICAL History Beginner
Six survivors of the Hiroshima bombing describe their experiences in the immediate aftermath. Essential reading before any nuclear preparedness planning — replaces abstract statistics with human reality. Shows what actually happened to people at varying distances from ground zero, the patterns of injury, how the community organized, and what effective and ineffective responses looked like. Originally published in The New Yorker.
Endurance: Shackleton's Incredible Voyage
Alfred Lansing — 1959
CRITICAL History Beginner
28 men stranded in Antarctica for 22 months after their ship was crushed by ice — and every one of them survived. A case study in extraordinary leadership, group psychology, morale management, decision-making under impossible conditions, and the human capacity for endurance. Shackleton's leadership methods are directly applicable to survival group management. Read for the leadership model.
Key lesson: Morale management, decision transparency, keeping people occupied, leader self-sacrifice
Alive: The Story of the Andes Survivors
Piers Paul Read — 1974
IMPORTANT History Beginner
A Uruguayan rugby team's survival for 72 days in the Andes after a plane crash, including the controversial decision to consume the deceased to survive. Detailed look at group dynamics, leadership, decision-making under extreme conditions, ethical choices in survival situations, and the psychology of hope. The famous case study that informed much of what we know about group survival psychology.
Five Days at Memorial: Life and Death in a Storm-Ravaged Hospital
Sheri Fink — 2013
IMPORTANT History Intermediate
Pulitzer Prize-winning investigation into the decisions made at a New Orleans hospital during Hurricane Katrina, when patients died under contested circumstances as medical staff made impossible triage decisions without power, supplies, or rescue. The most detailed examination in print of how medical systems fail under catastrophic conditions and how individual caregivers make life-and-death decisions without guidance. Essential reading for anyone who may provide medical care in a crisis.
Key lesson: System failure patterns, triage decisions under resource scarcity, moral injury in prolonged crises
