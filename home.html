What it is

Everything you'd want to know, working when nothing else does

One folder of plain HTML you open in any browser. It pairs a hand-written survival reference with an offline AI, a library of open reference books, calculators, and maps — all stored locally.

📚

27 sections + 29 books

Water, medical, energy, shelter, comms, navigation, agriculture, veterinary, NBC/EMP, and more — plus bundled manuals like Where There Is No Doctor and Bowditch's Navigator.

🤖

An AI that cites its sources

"Bunker Bot" runs on your own machine (Ollama or a llamafile) and answers from the bundled books, citing source + page. It won't invent drug doses.

🗺️

Offline maps + expansion

Pan and zoom real maps with zero signal, and one-click links to put all of Wikipedia and Project Gutenberg on your drive via Kiwix.

🔌

Runs on anything

Pure static HTML, zero dependencies, no build step. Works from a USB stick, an old laptop, a phone, or a Raspberry Pi.

🛠️

Questions

Good to know

Is it really free?

Yes — completely free to download, use, and share, forever. It's open source under CC BY-SA. There are no accounts, no ads, and no upsells. A donation button exists if you want to help with hosting costs, but it's entirely optional.

Does it actually work with no internet?

Yes. It's a folder of plain HTML that runs entirely offline — from a USB stick, an old laptop, a phone, or a Raspberry Pi. The optional AI runs on your own machine too. Nothing needs a connection once you've downloaded it.

Do I need to be technical?

No. Download, unzip, and double-click index.html — it opens in any browser like a normal website. The offline AI is optional and has a 2-minute setup if you want it.

Is the medical content safe to rely on?

It's a reference, not a substitute for professional care. The content is broad and AI-assisted, and the medical material has not yet been reviewed by a licensed clinician — so verify anything critical and seek qualified help whenever it's available. The AI cites its sources so you can check every claim.

What's the difference between Lite and Standard?

Lite (~1 MB) is the guide app and offline search — bring your own AI model. Standard (~560 MB) adds all 29 reference books, the AI search index so Bunker Bot can cite them, a regional offline map, and the terminal CLI.

Can I mirror or re-host it?

Please do — it's CC BY-SA. The full source is on GitHub, and there are torrent / IPFS options for censorship-resistant copies.
